Example · for builders

Build your founder playbook

You’re building something — so you don’t want to just watch a startup course, you want to turn it into principles you’ll actually reach for, and keep adding to as you go. Here’s a real corpus to start from, and the loop that makes it compound.

  1. Clone the starter folder, then open it

    stashbase-cs183b is a ready-made folder: full transcripts of all 20 lectures from Stanford CS183B — How to Start a Startup (the Y Combinator / Sam Altman course), plus a founder playbook distilled from them.

    git clone https://github.com/0-bingwu-0/stashbase-cs183b

    Then open the folder in StashBase. Its embeddings are prebuilt and ship with the repo, so StashBase can reuse them instead of re-embedding the whole course.

  2. Read the distilled playbook

    Open founder_playbook.html in StashBase. It’s ~60 founder questions, each answered as Principles → Decision Framework → Field Checklist → Traps to Avoid → Drawn From, cited back to the lectures. This is what a curated layer looks like sitting on top of 20 raw transcripts — the source kept read-only, the playbook derived from it.

  3. Ask in your own words

    Open the built-in Claude agent and just ask. It searches across the playbook and all 20 transcripts, and answers with sources you can open. Three to start, straight from the playbook:

    • How do I find a startup idea?
    • How do I know if I have product-market fit?
    • Should I worry about competitors and being copied?

  4. Make it yours

    The compounding part

    Don’t stop at the course. Drop your own material into transcripts/ — a YC essay, notes from a customer interview, a talk that stuck with you — and ask the agent to reconcile the playbook: extend an answer, or add a new question, always citing the new source (source always wins).

    Over weeks the playbook stops being “Sam Altman’s course” and becomes your operating manual — kept current by the agent as a side effect of the work you’re already doing.

One-time setup: search and the agent run on an OpenAI key — if you haven’t added one yet, it’s a quick step in Settings.
