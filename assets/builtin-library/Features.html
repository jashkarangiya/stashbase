What StashBase can do

The short version — open any line for the detail.

01Stash anything you see

Drag in files and folders, paste or drop images and PDFs, import a repo, or record your screen. Only what you choose to keep enters your memory — nothing is collected behind your back.

Good to know: to bring in a repo, clone it yourself and import the folder.
02It reads more than text

PDFs are extracted, images are run through OCR, and screen recordings are turned into structured, searchable notes — with the original kept alongside.

Good to know: screen recordings use Gemini (needs a key). Still images currently capture their text layer only.
03Find it by meaning or by word

Hybrid retrieval combines semantic (vector) search with exact keyword matching, so you find things whether or not you remember the wording.

Good to know: semantic search needs an OpenAI key. Without one, keyword search still works.
04Open to every AI client

Your whole knowledge base is exposed over MCP, so Claude, ChatGPT, Codex and others can read it directly — even when the app is closed.

05Agents keep it tidy

Agents organize, link, and update your notes as a side effect of the work you ask them to do — not as a chore you schedule.

Good to know: always under your direction. Nothing runs autonomously in the background.
06Yours, local, and portable

Everything is plain files on your disk, in open formats, organized as folders. Search across the whole library or narrow the scope to one folder. Take it anywhere — it’s never locked to us.

Some of these need a key to switch on — you can add one anytime in Settings.
